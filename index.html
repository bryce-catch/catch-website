About CATCH

CATCH is committed to making play accessible to all children.

Our Story

CATCH began as a simple idea: what if we could modify toys to make them accessible for children with disabilities? What started as a small project at UNC Chapel Hill has grown into a thriving nonprofit organization serving families across North Carolina.

Our founders recognized that while the toy market offers countless options for a wide range of children, children with disabilities still encounter barriers to play. Traditional toys may be difficult to activate, lack sensory feedback, or simply not be designed with accessibility in mind.

Today, we've adapted hundreds of toys, trained dozens of volunteers, and most importantly, brought smiles to countless children's faces. Our work goes beyond simple modifications â€“ we're building a more inclusive world where every child can participate in the fundamental human experience of play.

The Impact of Accessible Play

Play is not just fun â€“ it's fundamental to child development. Through play, children develop motor skills, cognitive abilities, social connections, and emotional resilience. When children with disabilities can't access traditional toys, they miss out on these crucial developmental opportunities.

Our adapted toys level the playing field, giving children with disabilities the same opportunities for growth, learning, and joy that all children deserve. We've seen children light up when they can independently activate a toy for the first time, families bond over shared play experiences, and communities become more inclusive and understanding.
